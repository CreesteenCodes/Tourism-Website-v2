About Mabini, Batangas

Mabini, Batangas is a peaceful coastal town famous for its clear waters, rocky beaches, and top-rated diving spots in Anilao. It’s named after national hero Apolinario Mabini, known as the “Brain of the Revolution.” What used to be a small fishing community has grown into a popular destination for divers, tourists, and nature lovers because of its rich marine life and relaxing atmosphere.

Top Attractions

Discover the Beauty of Mabini, Batangas

Camp Netanya Resort

A Santorini-inspired resort in Mabini with white-and-blue architecture, ocean views, and an infinity pool.

Mt. Gulugod Baboy

A beginner-friendly hike with rolling hills and panoramic views — popular for day hikes, camping, and sunrise or sunset watching.

The Cathedral Rock

A well-known dive site with rock formations shaped like church pillars, marked by a cross and surrounded by vibrant marine life.

Getting to Our Destination

By Car:

Drive from Manila via SLEX and STAR Tollway, then exit at Batangas City. Follow the road to Anilao/Mabini. The trip takes about 2.5–3 hours.

By Bus:

Take a bus to Batangas Grand Terminal (from Buendia or Cubao), then ride a jeepney or tricycle to Mabini.

Local Transport:

Within Mabini, tricycles serve as the main land transport to resorts and trailheads, while boats are used to reach dive sites and nearby islands.

Best Time to Visit:

November to May offers the best weather—clear skies, calm seas, and ideal diving conditions.
